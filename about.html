About

Who I am
and what I am doing

I am a Nairobi-based researcher and Computer Science graduate working at the intersection of natural language processing, educational assessment, and AI governance in African contexts. My work begins with a specific observation: the AI tools entering Kenyan classrooms were not built for Kenyan students.

"The question is not whether AI will be in African schools. It is whether it will serve African students — or merely be applied to them."

I am building the evidence and infrastructure this question demands: annotated datasets of Kenyan multilingual student writing, audit methodologies for AI educational tools, and NLP resources for Swahili, Kenyan English, and Sheng. This work lives at an intersection that has not yet been claimed. I think of that as an invitation, not a warning.
